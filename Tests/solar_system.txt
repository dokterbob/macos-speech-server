The solar system formed approximately four and a half billion years ago from a giant molecular cloud of gas and dust. At its center, the Sun contains more than ninety-nine percent of the total mass of the solar system. The eight planets orbit the Sun at varying distances, from Mercury, the closest, to Neptune, the farthest. Earth, our home planet, is the third planet from the Sun and the only known world to harbor life. The Moon, Earths only natural satellite, stabilizes our planets axial tilt and drives the ocean tides. Beyond Neptune lies the Kuiper Belt, a region of icy bodies and dwarf planets including Pluto. Scientists continue to explore these distant reaches with robotic spacecraft, uncovering new details about the origins and evolution of our cosmic neighborhood.
